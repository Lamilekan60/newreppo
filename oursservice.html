Pedicure Types

A classic pedicure is a traditional foot treatment that involves shaping, grooming, and pampering your feet. This timeless beauty ritual typically includes a series of steps designed to leave your feet looking and feeling their best.

  • Foot Soak: The treatment begins with a warm foot soak to soften the skin and nails. This relaxing step helps to loosen dirt and impurities, preparing your feet for the rest of the treatment.
  • Nail Shaping: Next, your toenails are trimmed and shaped to your desired length and style. This is an important step, as properly shaped nails can enhance the overall appearance of your feet.
  • Cuticle Care: Your cuticles are gently pushed back and trimmed to remove any excess skin. This helps to prevent hangnails and keeps your cuticles looking neat and tidy.
  • Callus Removal: Any calluses or dead skin on your heels and toes are carefully removed to leave your skin feeling smooth and soft.
  • Moisturizing: Finally, a nourishing moisturizer is applied to hydrate your skin and lock in moisture. This leaves your feet feeling soft, smooth, and pampered.

A spa pedicure is a comprehensive foot treatment that involves a series of steps designed to pamper and rejuvenate your feet. This luxurious experience typically begins with a warm foot soak infused with essential oils or salts to soften and relax the feet. A gel pedicure is a type of nail treatment that involves applying gel polish to the toenails. This popular nail technique is known for its long-lasting finish and high-shine appearance.

  • Nail Preparation: The toenails are prepared for the gel polish application by cleaning and shaping them.
  • Base Coat Application: A base coat is applied to protect the nails and promote adhesion.
  • Gel Polish Application: A gel polish is applied in thin layers, allowing for a precise and even finish.
  • Curing: Each layer of gel polish is cured under a UV or LED light to create a strong and durable finish.
  • Finishing Touches: A top coat is applied to seal the gel polish and add shine.

An acrylic pedicure is a type of nail enhancement that involves applying acrylic material to the toenails. This popular nail technique is known for its durability and versatility.

  • Nail Preparation: The toenails are prepared for the acrylic application by cleaning and shaping them. This step ensures a smooth and even surface for the acrylic.
  • Acrylic Application: A liquid monomer is mixed with a powder to create a paste, which is then applied to the nail. The acrylic is carefully molded and shaped to achieve the desired shape and length.
  • Shaping: The acrylic is shaped and molded to achieve the desired shape and length. This step requires precision and skill to create a natural-looking nail.
  • Finishing Touches: The acrylic is filed and buffed to create a smooth finish. This step helps to remove any imperfections and create a high-shine finish.

A French pedicure is a classic nail technique that involves painting a white tip on the end of the toenail. This timeless and elegant style is known for its simplicity and sophistication.

  • Nail Preparation: The toenails are prepared for the French pedicure by cleaning and shaping them. This step ensures a smooth and even surface for the polish..
  • Base Coat Application: A base coat is applied to protect the nails and promote adhesion. This helps the polish stick to the nail and prevents staining.
  • Gel Polish Application: A gel polish is applied in thin layers, allowing for a precise and even finish.
  • White Tip Application: A white polish is carefully painted onto the tip of the nail, creating a crisp and clean line. This step requires precision and skill to achieve a perfect, even line.
  • Finishing Touches: A top coat is applied to seal the polish and add shine. This final step helps to protect the polish and give the nails a glossy finish.
